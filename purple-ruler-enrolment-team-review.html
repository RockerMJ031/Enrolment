Review the new product-led enrolment experience

Use this page to keep the review focused on the biggest commercial, operational, and client-experience decisions. This is a briefing-first review: the team can review the prototype this week, hear the walkthrough next week, and then lock the final direction.

By the end of the session, the team should leave with one of three calls: approve the direction, approve with changes, or reject a core assumption.

Priority Review List

P0

Commercial model is understandable before submission

The prototype must make pricing and commercial consequences visible before the client submits.

  • Does the quote clearly show ex-VAT total, VAT @20%, and total inc. VAT?
  • Does Cat B show the £200 setup fee trigger and waiver logic clearly?
  • Does Cat D show the 30% low-volume uplift clearly and not feel like a hidden penalty?
  • Does custom timing outside :00 or :30 clearly trigger +20% on the affected group?
Decision required Pricing risk
P0

Payment and Accept Quote policy is commercially safe

The team needs to decide how much commitment is required before delivery preparation starts.

  • For Academy, should card subscription/payment be the default and strongest path?
  • For Blueprint, Compass, and Distinction, is Accept Quote enough before delivery preparation?
  • Should UK schools be allowed PO to follow, or must quote acceptance happen before start?
  • Should Local Authorities have a different approved PO/framework pathway?
Cash flow UK procurement
P0

Product boundaries prevent bad-fit requests

The enrolment flow should reduce complaints and delivery drag by pushing clients toward the right product.

  • Can a school/LA immediately tell the difference between Academy, Blueprint, Compass, and Distinction?
  • Is Distinction clearly outcome-focused tutoring, not unlimited bespoke schooling?
  • Is Compass clearly SEND-informed tutoring, not therapy?
  • Is Academy clearly standardised AP delivery, not a bespoke curriculum service?
Complaint prevention Product-led enrolment
P1

Schedule rules reduce teacher waste

The B/D group schedule UI should make stable delivery easier and costly complexity visible.

  • Are :00 and :30 timings treated as standard and not charged extra?
  • Is custom timing outside :00/:30 visibly priced as +20%?
  • Should Week A / Week B alternating schedules be blocked, charged, or routed to manual review?
  • Does the group model make teacher allocation more predictable?
Ops cost Teacher utilisation
P1

Client can complete the request without giant-form fatigue

The prototype must feel faster and clearer than the old Lark intake process.

  • Can the client choose product, paste students, configure needs, and review quote without losing context?
  • Are Add student and Add group actions obvious enough?
  • Does the right-side quote help decision-making rather than distract?
  • Does the review step let users fix mistakes quickly?
Client UX Friction reduction
P1

Add-ons feel like entitlements, not random extras

Add-ons should turn high-effort client behaviours into visible commercial choices.

  • Are add-ons framed around client pain points, not internal operations?
  • Do selected add-ons appear in quote sheet with price and remove action?
  • Are pass-through exam centre fees clearly separate from Purple Ruler fees?
  • Are the current add-on catalogues too broad, too narrow, or right for phase one?
Margin protection Expectation management
P2

Portal relationship after submission is credible

The submitted state should feel like the start of an ongoing service relationship, not the end of a form.

  • Are the client-visible statuses simple enough: Submitted, In Review, Action Needed, Preparing Delivery, Live, Closed?
  • Does the submitted page make portal follow-up obvious?
  • What exactly should trigger Action Needed?
  • Which email notifications are essential for phase one?
Portal Retention
P2

Data collected is complete enough for operations

The prototype should collect enough data at first submission to avoid back-and-forth.

  • Are required student fields sufficient for onboarding?
  • Does B/D group-level data support scheduling and teacher allocation?
  • Are attachments such as EHCP and scheme of work missing from this prototype review?
  • Can the JSON payload map cleanly into AnyCross/Lark?
Ops handover Data model
P3

Visual polish and Wix implementation details

These matter, but should not dominate the first commercial review.

  • Does the page feel aligned with purpleruler.com?
  • Are typography, spacing, and quote-sheet density appropriate for school/LA users?
  • Which parts should be native Wix elements vs custom Velo/HTML components?
  • What can be simplified for the first Wix Studio implementation?

Key Decisions To Capture

Decision Why it matters Recommended call
Academy payment model Academy should be the low-friction, standardised provision engine. Prefer card payment/subscription before start.
Accept Quote vs card for B/C/D UK schools and LAs often use procurement/PO workflows. Allow Accept Quote, but require quote acceptance before start.
Week A / Week B schedule policy Alternating timetables can waste teacher capacity and create admin load. Discuss whether to block, price, or manual-review.
Cat B setup fee Small bespoke courses are costly to set up and hard to scale. Keep £200 if under 10 groups and under 200h.
Cat D low-volume uplift Distinction needs stronger boundaries to reduce mismatch and complaints. Keep 30% uplift under 200h, capped at 200h equivalent.
Custom timing uplift Non-standard minutes create teacher scheduling complexity. Keep :00/:30 standard; other minutes +20%.

Review Cadence

This week Team reviews the prototype and this priority list asynchronously. Capture comments, risks, and objections without trying to resolve everything immediately.
Next week Walk through the enrolment model, explain the commercial logic, and answer questions around pricing, payment, schedule rules, and Wix implementation.
Finalise Lock the agreed product boundaries, quote/payment model, surcharge rules, and next Wix build scope.
Mode Inform first, discuss second, decide third. This keeps the review from becoming a long live design-by-committee session.

Do Not Get Stuck On

  • Pixel-perfect visual polish before commercial rules are approved.
  • Full production Stripe implementation details.
  • Every Lark field mapping during the first review.
  • US district/school edge cases; this prototype is UK school/LA first.

Review Lens

The strongest version of this prototype should reduce admin load, protect teacher capacity, clarify client expectations, and preserve revenue even if Purple Ruler accepts fewer low-quality requests.
